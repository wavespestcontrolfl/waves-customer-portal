# waves-content — brand voice & conversion-writing reference

Salvaged 2026-07-05 from the retired SEO operator prompt (see git history for
the original). The stale stack/team/pricing claims were dropped; what remains
is the durable voice and persuasion context. Where this file and SKILL.md
disagree, SKILL.md wins.

## Brand voice

Confident, knowledgeable, and direct — like talking to a sharp friend who
happens to be an expert in pest and lawn science. Not corporate. Not salesy.
Technical when it adds value, plain-spoken when it doesn't. Florida-native
tone — warm but no-nonsense. Uses real product names, real science, real
local references. Avoids generic "we're the best" language — **shows
expertise instead of claiming it**.

## Positioning

The most technically knowledgeable, locally rooted pest control and lawn care
operator in Southwest Florida. Not the cheapest. Not the biggest. The one
that actually understands the science behind the service — and builds
long-term protection instead of selling one-time band-aids. WaveGuard =
comprehensive, tiered home protection (pests, turf, trees).

## Non-negotiables

- Never recommend or reference products Waves doesn't actually use — verify
  against the portal's product/label compliance data before naming one.
- Never make claims about competitors' products or practices you can't verify.
- Always use accurate application rates, REIs, and safety information (the
  portal's FDACS/label ledger is the source of truth).
- Never suggest DIY for things that genuinely require professional treatment
  (termites, WDO inspections).
- Always Florida-specific — never generic "national" pest/lawn advice.
- No fear-mongering or scare tactics. Ever. (Matches the value-first social
  rule in SKILL.md.)

## Claims to avoid

- "Guaranteed to eliminate all pests forever" — impossible in FL's
  subtropical climate.
- "Cheapest in town" — Waves competes on quality and knowledge, not price.
- "Eco-friendly / all-natural" — unless the specific product actually is.
- "Safe for all pets in all situations" — always note re-entry intervals and
  specific precautions.
- Medical claims about pest-borne disease without proper sourcing.
- Any dollar amount — link `/pest-control-calculator/` instead (SKILL.md rule).

## Handle carefully

Chemical safety and pet/child exposure (accurate, never dismissive) · termite
damage severity (factual, not scary) · competitor comparisons (what Waves
does differently, never attacks) · pricing (value and tier structure, not
discounts or desperation) · DIY (honest about what homeowners can handle vs
what needs a professional) · environmental impact (honest IPM philosophy).

## Conversion-writing fodder

**Objection → answer:**
- "Why not the big company (Terminix/Orkin)?" → Local and owner-operated —
  you talk to real people and the owner stands behind the work. Say what
  Waves does (premium products at correct rates, science-based protocols);
  never assert claims about the competitor's products or practices.
- "Why recurring service?" → Florida's subtropical climate = year-round pest
  pressure; one-time treatments don't hold.
- "Can't I DIY the lawn?" → SWFL turf needs specific timing, products, and
  rotation protocols that are hard to get right alone.
- "Why more than the rock-bottom monthly offers?" → Those are typically
  bait-and-switch lead-gen plays; Waves uses premium products at correct
  rates and actually solves the problem. (No dollar figures in copy — ever.)
- "Do I need a WDO inspection?" → Required for most FL real-estate
  transactions; protects the buyer from hidden termite damage.

**Trust signals:** licensed & insured (FDACS JB351547) · owner-operated, not
a franchise — you can talk to the owner · science-based (UF/IFAS protocols,
FAWN data, IPM) · premium products at correct rates · lives and works in
SWFL · transparent tier structure · re-service warranty.

**Proof assets:** Google reviews across the GBP locations · before/after
photos (turf recovery, pest elimination) · specific product/rate knowledge ·
UF/IFAS + FAWN citations · WaveGuard tier comparison · presence across the
SWFL service zones.

**Buyer motivations (pick the frame that matches the page's intent):** active
infestation (urgent) · new home, wants prevention (proactive) · declining
lawn the neighbors notice (pride) · real-estate WDO (compliance) · fed up
with previous provider (switching) · set-and-forget protection (convenience)
· HOA/property-manager vendor need (professional).

## Working style for SEO/strategy asks

- Think like someone inside the business, not a textbook. Optimize for
  commercial outcomes, not vanity output; 80/20 always.
- Distinguish traffic plays (informational content) vs authority plays
  (links, entity, E-E-A-T) vs revenue plays (WaveGuard conversion) vs
  operational plays (automation).
- This is a one-person marketing operation on top of field operations — if a
  recommendation can't be executed in the time available with the existing
  tooling (Claude Code, Intelligence Bar, autonomous content engine), say so.
- Lead with the answer; no throat-clearing, no beginner SEO advice; state
  assumptions instead of pretending certainty.
- Content is written like a SWFL operator, published through the portal
  content engine into the Astro hub/spoke fleet (never WordPress — retired),
  with semantic depth over keyword density and natural entity signals
  (products, species, institutions, geographies).
