# Bed Bug Treatment Protocol — Tech Reference

**Audience:** field technician. **Scope:** residential/light-commercial bed bug jobs (SW Florida — Manatee / Sarasota / Charlotte).
**Method classes:** chemical/IPM, heat, or hybrid. Bed bug is a **specialty service** — separate prep, pricing, and follow-up. Do not merge it into a general pest workflow.

> **Two things govern everything below:** (1) **the product label is the law** — where this doc and a current label disagree, follow the label; (2) bed bugs are beaten by **layered methods + client prep + follow-up**, never a single spray. Anyone promising "one treatment, done" (without heat) is wrong.

---

## The kit — know why each item is in the bag

### Current program (baseline — what's in the truck today)

| Product | Active (IRAC group) | Role in the job |
|---|---|---|
| **Crossfire** | Clothianidin (4A) + Metofluthrin (3A) | **Primary adulticide + ovicide.** Dual-MOA, non-repellent, kills pyrethroid-resistant strains, ovicidal on contact. Label permits mattress seams/tufts — **let dry before encasing/re-use**. |
| **Alpine WSG** | Dinotefuran (4A) | **Secondary residual / flush** for voids, cracks, perimeter, adjacent-room harborage. Non-repellent, low-odor, reduced-risk. |
| **Gentrol** | (S)-Hydroprene (7A IGR) | **Cycle-breaker.** Sterilizes adults, blocks nymph maturation, disrupts egg viability. Tank-mix with Crossfire **or** deploy Point Source discs at harborage. |
| **Heat** (offered tier) | Thermal remediation | Kills **all life stages incl. eggs in one session**, penetrates harborage. **Leaves zero residual** — must be paired with a residual. |

**Crossfire mix:** `~13 mL per quart of water` — **confirm against the current label before use.** One quart typically treats 1–2 beds.

> ### ⚠ Resistance-management note — read this
> Crossfire's **clothianidin** and Alpine's **dinotefuran** are **both Group 4A neonicotinoids.** They are **not** a rotation of each other — running both is one mode of action twice. Your genuine MOA diversity is **metofluthrin (3A) + Gentrol IGR (7A) + physical methods.** Treat Crossfire + Alpine as a single neonic backbone, and lean on the non-neonic layers (steam, desiccant, IGR) to carry resistance management.

### Gold-standard upgrades (adopt as stocked — see roadmap at the bottom)

These are **recommendations**, not current baseline steps. They are called out inline below with the **★ GOLD** tag.

| Upgrade | Active (IRAC) | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **CimeXa** (silica-gel desiccant) | Physical (no MOA — resistance-proof) | **Months-long** residual in voids liquids can't reach (frame tubing, outlets, wall voids). Highest-value single addition. |
| **Phantom II** (chlorfenapyr) | Group 13 | A residual with a **genuinely different mechanism** than the neonics. Slow-acting on purpose — carried back to harborage. Fixes the two-neonic gap. |
| **Aprehend** (*Beauveria bassiana*) | Biopesticide (UNB) | Fungal-spore barrier; bugs pick it up and **spread it to harborage-mates**. ~3-month residual, resistance-proof. Specialist-tier differentiator. |
| **Interceptors + encasements** | Physical | Monitoring and verification tool. Encasements convert the mattress/box spring into a sealed, inspectable surface. |
| **K9 detection** | — | Highest-accuracy detection and post-treatment clearance confirmation. |

## Visit 1 — Inspection & classification

**Goal: confirm, map, and classify method before any customer promise or treatment.**

- [ ] Inspect all sleeping/resting zones: mattress seams, box spring, bed frame, headboard, baseboards, furniture seams, outlet/switch plates, luggage zones, **and adjacent rooms**.
- [ ] Document: live bugs, cast skins, eggs, fecal spotting, bites reported, **rooms affected**, prep status, occupancy type.
- [ ] **Photograph evidence before quoting or treating.**
- [ ] Deploy **interceptors** under bed/furniture legs — this is your baseline count and later your clearance tool.
- [ ] **Classify the method: chemical/IPM, heat, or hybrid.** Pricing and scope follow the method.
- [ ] Send prep instructions; confirm the customer can comply.
- [ ] Escalate severe, multi-unit, or heavily cluttered jobs for office review.

> Inspection must identify **rooms affected** and **treatment method** before any promise. Keep quote-required cases out of the generic pest flow.

## Visit 2 — Treatment

### Gate — do not treat if prep isn't done
Confirm: linens/clothing **hot-dried and bagged**, clutter reduced, furniture pulled off walls, items **not** relocated to other rooms (that spreads them). Not prepped → **reschedule, don't treat.**

### Chemical / hybrid sequence
1. **HEPA vacuum** live bugs and eggs from seams, frames, and edges → bag and **discard outside immediately**.
2. **Steam** mattress seams/tufts, box spring, headboard, upholstered furniture, and baseboards where liquid isn't suitable (contact kill, including eggs).
3. **Crossfire** (`~13 mL/qt`, confirm label) into cracks/crevices/harborage: bed frame, headboard joints, baseboards, wall–floor junction, carpet tack strip, furniture seams. Mattress seams/tufts **per label — let dry before encasing**.
4. **Gentrol** — tank-mix with Crossfire, **or** place Point Source discs at harborage.
5. **★ GOLD — CimeXa:** *light* film into voids (outlet/switch plates, wall voids, bed-frame tubing, behind baseboards). **Light dust only** — visible piles repel bugs.
6. **Alpine WSG** residual for broader voids / perimeter / adjacent-room harborage as needed.
   - **★ GOLD:** where stocked, use **Phantom II (chlorfenapyr, Grp 13)** as the primary crack-and-crevice residual and drop Alpine to a flush/secondary — this gives a true 3A + 4A + 13 + 7A spread.
7. **★ GOLD — Encase** mattress + box spring after treatment dries; **leave encasements on 12+ months** (bed bugs survive ~a year without feeding). Leave **interceptors** in place.
8. **★ GOLD — Aprehend** barrier strips at harborage approaches where adopted (refrigerated storage + dedicated sprayer + training required).

### Heat / hybrid execution
- Stage room to **~130–135°F ambient** so the harborage **core** clears ~120°F+.
- Place **remote wireless sensors *in* harborage** — not just ambient air.
- **Fans / air movement** to eliminate cold spots; watch for bugs fleeing to cool edges → treat the perimeter.
- Remove/protect heat-sensitive items: aerosols, pressurized containers, medications, electronics, candles/wax, vinyl.
- **Always follow heat with a residual** (Crossfire perimeter + CimeXa voids). Heat alone fails on **re-introduction**, not the original bugs — critical in our rental/snowbird market.

### Document before leaving
Rooms treated, products + rates, zones/harborage addressed, inaccessible areas, **re-entry timing (until dry)**, and the **follow-up date**.

## Visit 3 — Follow-up & clearance (SWFL cadence)

- **Return ~10–14 days out.** Warm FL means eggs hatch in ~6–10 days and infestations rebound fast; snowbird/rental turnover raises reintroduction risk. **Minimum 2 treatments, often 3.**
- Reinspect treated **and adjacent** rooms; **read the interceptors**.
- Spot-treat live activity per label; reinforce laundry / bagging / clutter / luggage precautions.
- Escalate ongoing activity for method review or added scope.

> **Clearance is defined by monitoring, not by eyeball.** Declare clear only on **zero interceptor activity across a 2–4 week window** — **★ GOLD:** confirm with a **K9 detection** pass where available.

---

## Safety / PPE / re-entry

- PPE per label: gloves, eye protection; **respirator when applying dusts**.
- Occupants and pets **out during application**; re-entry when surfaces are **dry** (typically ~2–4 hrs — follow label).
- Cover aquariums / turn off aerators per label. Extra care with infants, elderly, and immunocompromised occupants.
- **Never exceed label on sleeping surfaces** — the encasement, not chemical saturation, mediates sleeping-surface contact.
- Do not apply to mattresses, furniture, or belongings unless the **label explicitly allows** that use.

## Adoption roadmap (owner reference)

The baseline steps above run entirely on the current kit. The **★ GOLD** upgrades, ranked by impact:

1. **CimeXa desiccant → make it a core step.** Cheap, resistance-proof, months of residual where liquids can't go. Do first.
2. **Phantom II (chlorfenapyr) → true MOA rotation.** Ends the two-neonic residual gap.
3. **Encasements 12+ months + interceptor-based clearance → verification discipline.** No new chemistry, immediate rigor.
4. **Aprehend + K9 → specialist tier.** Real cost/logistics (refrigeration, sprayer, training / dog); this is what backs a defensible "highest-level bed bug service" claim.
